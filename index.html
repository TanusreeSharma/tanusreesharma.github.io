[Oakland] 2023
AI systems increasingly stand between people and the everyday systems they interact with. Most of the time, we don't get much say in whether that exchange is fair and interaction is safe. That can sound abstract, but it shows up in very concrete places — a blind user verifying their identity for government benefits, voice actors’ recordings get cloned without consent, teenagers navigating a chatbot that wasn't really built with them in mind, a community trying to get a say in how an AI model is governed.
Tanusree approaches this as a two-sided problem: addressing AI safety risks while using AI to solve long-standing security challenges. She works at the intersection of Security, AI, and HCI. She leads HIVE Lab (Human Signal in Intelligence, Verification, and Empowerment) with a guiding question: How can we design secure systems that improve the quality of human interaction and human data in digital ecosystems? Her group's work draws on information and computer sciences (privacy and security, HCI, ML) and the social sciences in …
Designing systems people can trust.
